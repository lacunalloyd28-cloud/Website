De La Salle John Bosco College

A PAASCU-accredited Lasallian district school located in Mangagoy, Bislig City, Surigao del Sur.

Our Story

Rooted in Mangagoy, guided by faith.

Established in 1963 by the Don Bosco Fathers, the school's administration and supervision was formally turned over to the De La Salle Brothers in 1977. DLSJBC now offers complete pre-school, basic education, senior high school, college, and TESDA-accredited TVET courses.

DLSJBC's foundation traces back to the Bislig Bay Elementary School, founded in the mid-1950s for the dependents of the Bislig Bay Lumber Company (BBLCI) — forerunner of PICOP, once one of the largest paper mill companies in Asia. In 1961 the company turned over management to Fr. Alberto Grol, who converted it into a parochial school open to the wider community.

In 1999, John Bosco School became John Bosco College with the opening of its College Department, and in February 2007 it was formally welcomed as the 18th district school of De La Salle Philippines — taking on its present name, De La Salle John Bosco College.
