Process

Enrollment Procedure

01

New Kindergarten

  1. See the Guidance-in-charge for the entrance exam / interview.
  2. Secure the Eligibility Form from the Principal's Office.
  3. Present PSA Birth Certificate and Eligibility Form to the Registrar.
  4. Proceed to the Finance Office for tuition leveling.
  5. Pay the enrollment fee at the cashier.
  6. Return to the Registrar's Office.
02

New Grade 1 – Grade 12

  1. Present admission requirements at the Registrar's Office.
  2. Secure and accomplish the Registration Form.
  3. Proceed to the Finance Office for tuition leveling.
  4. Pay the enrollment fee at the cashier.
  5. Return to the Registrar's Office.
03

Continuing Students

  1. Present original report card to the Registrar's Office.
  2. Secure and accomplish the Registration Form.
  3. Proceed to the Finance Office for tuition leveling.
  4. Pay the enrollment fee at the cashier.
  5. Return to the Registrar's Office.
About Us

A Lasallian Legacy Since 1963

From a company-run elementary school to a full Lasallian district college.

Our History

The De La Salle John Bosco College (DLSJBC) is a PAASCU-accredited Lasallian district school located in Mangagoy, Bislig City, Surigao del Sur. It was established in 1963 by the Don Bosco Fathers, and administration was formally turned over to the De La Salle Brothers in 1977.

The school traces its roots to the Bislig Bay Elementary School, founded in the mid-1950s for the dependents of the Bislig Bay Lumber Company Incorporated. It grew from John Bosco School into John Bosco College in 1999, and in 2007 was formally accepted as the 18th district school of De La Salle Philippines, taking on its present name.

Today, DLSJBC offers complete pre-school, basic education, senior high school, college, and TESDA-accredited TVET programs — serving as the leading Lasallian and Catholic institution in Bislig City and the CARAGA region.

🛡

Our Identity

DLSJBC's crest features a white shield with a tower and cross, symbolizing the school's Catholic identity, and the Chi-Rho in the upper corner as a sign of peace.

🕊

Lasallian Values

Guided by the Lasallian mission of Faith, Service, and Communion, the school forms students holistically — academically, spiritually, and socially.
